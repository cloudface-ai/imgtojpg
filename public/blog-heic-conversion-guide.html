Complete Guide: Converting HEIC to JPG

Learn everything about HEIC files and how to convert them to JPG format. HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is Apple's modern image format that offers better compression than JPEG while maintaining quality.

What is HEIC?

HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is Apple's modern image format that was introduced with iOS 11. It offers significantly better compression than JPEG while maintaining similar or better image quality.

Why Convert HEIC to JPG?

While HEIC is excellent for Apple devices, it's not universally supported across all platforms and applications. Converting to JPG ensures compatibility with:

  • Web browsers and websites
  • Social media platforms
  • Email clients
  • Windows and Android devices
  • Photo editing software

Technical Details

HEIC uses the HEVC (H.265) codec for compression, which is up to 50% more efficient than JPEG's compression algorithm. This means smaller file sizes without sacrificing quality.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between HEIC and JPG?

HEIC (High Efficiency Image Container) is Apple's modern format that offers 50% better compression than JPG while maintaining the same quality. However, JPG has universal compatibility across all devices and platforms, while HEIC is primarily supported by Apple devices.

Why do my iPhone photos show as HEIC files?

Starting with iOS 11, Apple began using HEIC format by default for photos taken with iPhone cameras. This provides better compression and quality, but the files aren't compatible with older devices or many web platforms, which is why conversion to JPG is often necessary.
